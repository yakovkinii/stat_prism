A Pearson correlation coefficient was calculated to assess the relationship between the variables «How old are you?», «What is your monthly income? (USD)», and «What was your test score?». The correlation between 'What is your monthly income? (USD)' and 'How old are you?' was not significant, r(33) = -.13, p = .454, 95% CI [-.44, .21]. The correlation between 'What was your test score?' and 'How old are you?' was not significant, r(34) = .02, p = .909, 95% CI [-.31, .35]. The correlation between 'What was your test score?' and 'What is your monthly income? (USD)' was not significant, r(35) = .11, p = .512, 95% CI [-.22, .42].
